About Me

I hold a master’s degree in Clinical Mental Health Counseling from Sam Houston State University (SHSU) where I graduated in 2018. SHSU is accredited through Council for Accreditation and Counseling and Related Educational Programs (CACREP).

I have a Bachelor’s of Science degree in Wildlife and Fisheries Science with a Psychology minor from Texas A&M University where I graduated in 2012.

You might think, weird, a wildlife and fisheries degree with a psych minor, and now a therapist? Well, no one is above burnout at work. Unlike you, I didn’t reach out for help at the time. I loved many aspects of being a wildlife biologist. I really enjoyed researching the dunes sagebrush lizard.

I used my wildlife management knowledge while in an AmeriCorpse program Called American Conservation Experience (ACE) where I worked with parks to conduct fuels management to help reduce the severity of fires, habitat restoration, trail building and trail maintenance.

I worked with an environmental consulting firm where I helped protect desert tortoise.

I learned a lot as a field biologist. The most important thing was realizing I enjoyed helping people. I was there to listen, support, and help guide others in their time of need. However, I never reached out for help myself.

I became overwhelmed in a career where I felt my employer's morals and ethics didn't align with in my own. They were not actually looking out for the best interest of the wildlife we were there to protect.

If I reached out for help, maybe I would still be a field biologist, but I have found peace where life has brought me. This is where one journey ended and a new one began. I decided to go back to graduate school to become a counselor.

My vision has been to find a way to incorporate nature with therapy.
